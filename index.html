Why this exists

Built by one person. Made better by you.

Fixel is a free AI tool that diagnoses everyday household repair problems from a photo or a description, then tells you whether the job is safe to do yourself or you should call a professional. Describe what's wrong — a dripping tap, a running toilet, a sparking switch — or snap a photo, and it returns an honest verdict (Easy DIY, DIY with caution, or Call a pro), a guided step-by-step fix for the safe ones, and the exact words to send a tradesperson for the ones that aren't. It's built for first-home buyers and renters, runs in your browser with no signup, and always routes electrical, gas, and structural work to a licensed professional. It's a personal project by Ray Gunawan — not affiliated with Vercel, where it happens to be hosted.

I built Fixel because buying or renting your first home comes with a hundred small breakages nobody teaches you about — and the choice is usually an hour of YouTube roulette or paying a call-out fee to learn it was a $5 washer. This tool gives you the honest answer first: what it probably is, whether it's safe to touch, and exactly what to do or say next.

It's free, with no signup and no catch — a personal project, not a startup. What I need most right now is real feedback: try it on something actually broken in your place, then tell me where it nailed it and where it fell flat.
